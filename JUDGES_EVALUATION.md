## 📝 Detailed Rubric Breakdown

### 1. Problem Solving & Impact (Score: 14 / 20)
- **Problem Definition**: Addresses the lack of standardized digital tools for Ayurvedic herb identification and safety.
- **Solution Effectiveness**: Integrates identification with safety and dosha matching engines.
- **Rationale**: Practical for herbal enthusiasts, though clinical safety validation requires broader testing.

### 2. Innovation & Originality (Score: 14 / 20)
- **Uniqueness**: Combines plant vision AI, Ayurvedic dosha recommendations, and trade market feeds.
- **Novel Approach**: Parallel fallback chain using Gemini Vision and Plant.id v3.
- **Rationale**: Innovative combination of traditional medicine domain knowledge with modern LLMs.

### 3. Tech & Feasibility (Score: 15 / 20)
- **Technical Soundness**: Full-stack Next.js 14 App Router, TypeScript, Prisma ORM, and Supabase.
- **Implementation**: Robust API routing and serverless deployment structure.
- **Rationale**: Highly feasible and well-implemented architecture with multi-provider AI fallback chains.

### 4. Business Plan / Impact (Score: 13 / 20)
- **Scalability**: High digital scalability across web and mobile viewports.
- **Market Potential**: Serves wellness consumer markets, Ayurvedic practitioners, and trade vendors.
- **Rationale**: Clear utility model; needs further formal monetization details (e.g. B2B vendor API subscriptions).

### 5. Presentation & Pitch (Score: 14 / 20)
- **Clarity & Structure**: Complete documentation, clear architecture layout, and working live deployment.
- **User Engagement**: Modern UI featuring interactive tools, dosage sliders, and floating AI assistant.
- **Rationale**: High visual appeal and structured presentation of core capabilities.
